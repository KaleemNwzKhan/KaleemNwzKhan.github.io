Kaleem Nawaz Khan

Rochester, NY 14623

I am a Ph.D. student, working with Dr. Fawad Ahmad in the Networked Sensing Systems Lab, Department of Computer Science at Rochester Institute of Technology, New York, USA.

My research focuses on networked sensing, particularly in autonomous driving. I aim to improve the perception capabilities of autonomous vehicles by leveraging vehicle-to-vehicle and roadside unit-to-vehicle cooperation. This involves the real-time sharing and precise fusion of raw 3D data. Additionally, I work on 3D reconstruction, mapping, and tracking using neural radiance field (NeRF) technology.

I graduated with honors from the National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences (NUCES), Pakistan, where I earned my Bachelor’s degree in 2015 and Master’s degree in Computer Science in 2018. From 2019 to 2021, I led the research team at the Intelligent Information Processing Lab within the National Center for Artificial Intelligence (NCAI) in Peshawar, Pakistan.
